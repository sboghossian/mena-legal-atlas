# LinkedIn announcement — draft

Three versions, founder-direct, lowercase comfort, concrete numbers. Pick one and post when the Atlas is ready for public eyes (after a validation pass on the high-priority entries).

---

## v1 — Data-led (recommended)

we audited every legal-data source in the middle east and north africa.

118 sources cataloged across 24 jurisdictions in the worldwidelaw open corpus.
42 working. 76 blocked.

10 countries have zero working scrapers — including lebanon, saudi arabia, israel, iraq, iran.
yemen has no entries at all.

the blockers aren't missing code. they're cloudflare WAFs, geo-IP restrictions, third-party paywalls, login walls. the people who could open the door are inside the ministries, not on github.

so we built the MENA Legal Data Atlas — an open map of every gazette, every consolidated legislation portal, every apex court, every regulator rulebook across 25 MENA jurisdictions. with pointers for whoever wants to build on top.

researcher-compiled v0.1 lives at github.com/sboghossian/mena-legal-atlas. v0.2 pairs each entry with a local-jurisdiction lawyer validator.

if you're a lawyer in any MENA country and want your jurisdiction validated by you instead of by a researcher, reply to this post.

## v2 — Provocation

every country in the middle east publishes its laws online.
most of them are unscrapeable.

we measured it. of 118 MENA legal-data sources in the largest open corpus (worldwidelaw/legal-sources), only 36% actually work. lebanon, saudi arabia, israel, iraq, iran, palestine, sudan, somalia, comoros, yemen — all sit at zero.

"open data" in MENA is a status, not a reality. the data exists. the access doesn't.

so we made the map. MENA Legal Data Atlas — 25 jurisdictions, every authoritative source, every blocker, every unblock hint. CC BY 4.0. validation pass starting next.

→ github.com/sboghossian/mena-legal-atlas
