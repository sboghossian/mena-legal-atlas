# Validator brief — MENA Legal Data Atlas

*Send this to a prospective validator. One page. Everything they need to say yes or no.*

## What the Atlas is

An open map of every legal-data source in 25 MENA jurisdictions — official gazettes, consolidated legislation portals, apex courts, sectoral regulators. CC BY 4.0 licensed. Published as a structured YAML + machine-readable JSON + reading copy. Hosted at `github.com/sboghossian/mena-legal-atlas`.

The Atlas's value is that **a lawyer, a journalist, an engineer, or a researcher can land in a MENA country they don't practice in and immediately know where to look for primary law**. Today that information is folkloric — passed lawyer-to-lawyer. The Atlas makes it explicit.

## What we need from you

A 30-minute review of the entry for **one jurisdiction** you are licensed in.

The entry is a YAML block plus a rendered Markdown view. We will share both.

You verify the following fields for accuracy:
- `legal_system` — is the one-line classification correct?
- `languages` — complete and ordered correctly?
- `calendar`, `citation_style` — accurate for working lawyers in the jurisdiction?
- `official_gazette` — name, URL, format, publication cadence
- `consolidated` — are the named legislation portals authoritative? Are better ones missing?
- `apex_courts` — complete list? Any missing specialty tribunals?
- `key_regulators` — the sector regulators a practicing lawyer would care about?
- `notes` — anything factually wrong?

You do **not** need to:
- Verify legal correctness of any specific law's text
- Build scrapers or web tools
- Comment on policy
- Endorse the Atlas as a whole

## What you get in return

- Public credit on the entry: your name, firm/university, jurisdiction, bar number
- Co-authorship credit on the v1.1 launch announcement (LinkedIn, README, any blog or paper)
- The Atlas links to you, not the other way around — you can use this as a public-engagement / pro-bono credit
- A copy of the full Atlas dataset, ahead of the public v1.1 release

For non-academic validators, a thank-you fee may be offered case-by-case. This is not a paid service review by default; we are not asking you to opine on any legal question.

## What we are *not* asking

- Not asking you to opine on any specific legal question
- Not asking you to vouch for the correctness of any law's text
- Not asking you to validate any other jurisdiction
- Not asking for ongoing work after the one-time validation pass

## Time and turnaround

- One review pass: ≈ 30 minutes
- Round trip: 1-2 weeks from "yes" to entry-locked

## Liability

The Atlas is published as research, not legal advice. The validator credit attests that the entry accurately reflects where to **find** primary law in the jurisdiction — not that any law's content is interpreted correctly.
