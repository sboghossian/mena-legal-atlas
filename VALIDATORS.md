# Validators

The MENA Legal Data Atlas is researcher-compiled. Every jurisdiction entry needs **one local-jurisdiction lawyer** to validate that it accurately reflects the country's primary-source landscape.

This page exists so we know who validated what, and so anyone reading the Atlas can see who stands behind each entry.

## What validators do

A validator is a lawyer licensed in the relevant MENA jurisdiction. The validation pass is short (≈ 30 minutes per jurisdiction) and covers:

1. **Official gazette** — Is the named gazette current? Is the URL correct? Is the publication cadence right?
2. **Consolidated legislation** — Is the listed portal authoritative? Are there better ones missing?
3. **Apex courts** — Are the listed top-tier courts complete? Are there missing specialty tribunals that materially shape the country's case-law landscape (e.g. tax courts, administrative chambers, religious-personal-status courts)?
4. **Key regulators** — Are the listed sectoral regulators the right ones for a practitioner? Are there missing rulebook publishers?
5. **Open-text fields** — Is the `legal_system`, `calendar`, `citation_style`, `language(s)` description accurate?
6. **Sanctions / political flags** — For jurisdictions in conflict (LY, SY, YE, IQ, PS), is the parallel-corpus or bifurcation note accurate?

What validators **do not** do:
- Build scrapers
- Write commentary
- Vouch for the legal correctness of any specific law's text
- Validate other jurisdictions

## What validators get

- **Public credit** on the country entry: `validated_by: "Full Name, Firm or University, Jurisdiction, Bar Number"`
- **Co-authorship** on any related publication (academic paper, blog, talk)
- **Atlas v1.1 launch announcement** credits the validators by name on LinkedIn + project README
- (For non-academic validators) a thank-you fee — discussed individually; this is not a paid review by default

## How to volunteer

Open an issue titled `validator: <CC>` and include:
- Your full name + firm or university affiliation
- Your bar number / equivalent (private — for the maintainer)
- Your jurisdiction
- A 1-line statement that you are willing to put your name on the entry

The maintainer will reply with the validator brief and the link to the country entry.

Alternatively, email `stephane.boghossian@haqq.ai`.
